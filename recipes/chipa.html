Chipá

Original recipe: María Ángeles Fernández - Bon Viveur

Description

Chipá is a cheese bread made with cassava starch (also known as yuca or tapioca starch), typical in Paraguay, Ecuador, Argentina, and Brazil. Depending on where this bread is prepared, it may also be known as pan de yuca, Brazilian cheese bread, or pão de queijo. Making it is very simple. The only trick to getting the perfect chipá is to knead the dough thoroughly from the very beginning so that all the ingredients blend perfectly.

  • Prep Time: 30 mins
  • Cook Time: 15 mins
  • Total Time: 45 mins
  • Servings: 10 (20 pieces)

Ingredients

  • 300 g cassava, yuca, or tapioca starch
  • 100 ml milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 70 g butter (at room temperature)
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 120 g semi-cured cheese or grated Parmesan cheese
  • 60 g fresh mozzarella

Steps

  1. Mix and Knead: In a large bowl, combine the cassava starch, milk, eggs, butter, and salt. Knead the mixture together

  2. Add the Cheese: Incorporate the cheeses into the chipá dough and continue kneading until you achieve a smooth texture

  3. Shape and Bake: Preheat the oven to 180°C (with both top and bottom heat). Shape the dough into 20 equal-sized balls, place them on a baking sheet, and bake the chipá for 12–15 minutes

  4. Serve: Serve the chipá warm, ideally accompanied by your choice of dipping sauce
